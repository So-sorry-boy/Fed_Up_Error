Why Is My Screen Black?

Your computer may have run into a critical issue that prevents visuals from rendering on-screen. This issue, though often fixable, may be a sign that Windows, or possibly your monitor, has decided to ghost you. Don't worry ‚Äî we‚Äôre here to help before it leaves you on ‚Äúread‚Äù permanently.

Step 1: Check Display Connections
Ensure all monitor cables are securely connected to both the display and the PC. Sometimes even a slightly loose HDMI or DisplayPort cable can disrupt output, resulting in a black screen that looks like your PC just gave up. Try unplugging and replugging the cables. If possible, use a different cable or monitor to rule out hardware faults. A common mistake is assuming the display is the problem when it could be the port, or vice versa.

Step 2: Perform a Forced Restart
If the screen remains black, press and hold the power button for 10 seconds until the system shuts off completely. This can help reset any processes stuck in a boot loop or a failed wake-from-sleep state. After powering back on, listen for startup beeps or fan sounds to indicate hardware activity. This step eliminates temporary software stalls and can sometimes restore video signal instantly if caused by a crash mid-boot.
